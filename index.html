About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Maryland's Information School, where I work with Dr. Amanda Lazar at The Health Aging and Technology (THAT) Lab. My research sits in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), draws on Computer-Supported Cooperative Work (CSCW) and contributes to designing with older adults and people with cognitive impairments. Currently, I focus on understanding how videoconferencing technology can serve as meaningful additions to older adults’ lives as they navigate changes in aging and cognitive abilities. My work is published at top HCI venues, including CHI, CSCW, and ASSETS.

Prior to my PhD, I earned a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science from Virginia Tech, where I worked with Dr. Sang Won Lee on developing interactive systems to facilitate empathy among users.
